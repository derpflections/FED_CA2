Lee Hong Yi was a student of Swiss Cottage Secondary School from 2018 to 2021.

Hong Yi is a diligent student who will always give his best in all that he does and remains undaunted when faced with challenges. He knows his targets in life and is able to prioritise his commitments to achieve his personal goals. Hong Yi is also respectful towards his teachers and peers and is also sensitive towards the feelings of others around him. His jovial nature and his great sense of humour provided him with the ability to bond well and build good rapport with his teachers and peers. Being an efficient and responsible student, he was appointed the Audio-Visual Representative for his class from 2018 to 2021.

In 2020, Hong Yi also exhibited strong analytical skills and ability to navigate the complexities of international diplomacy through his participation as a delegate of the Swiss Model United Nations (SMUN) 2020. For his charismatic leadership, strategic negotiation skills, ability to critically analyse policies, and overall contribution to the conference, Hong Yi was awarded the Best Delegate of United Nations Human Rights Council (UNHRC). For his commitment to coming up with a comprehensive resolution and her ability to critically analyse policies, Hong Yi's committee, UNHRC, was awarded the Best Resolution in the conference. In order to enrich himself beyond the school experience, Hong Yi also participated in the River Valley High School Model United Nations (RVMUN) in 2021, where he had the opportunities to assume the roles of national delegates and debate on pertinent global issues. He also took part in the International Competition and Assessment for Schools (Mathematics) in 2018 and attained the Certificate of Credit.

Hong Yi was a dedicated member of the Publications and Media Club (PMC) from 2018 to 2021. In 2020, he was appointed the Logistics Head of the club. His responsibilities included the management of a team that oversaw the maintenance and management of the club's inventory of audio and visual equipment. A leader with foresight, he was often able to quickly identify challenges ahead of time and adapt to the situation swiftly for a positive outcome. For example, he revamped the Media Lab and implemented a new organisation system, which streamlined the workspace and increased the efficiency of the workflow of the club. Besides maintaining the upkeep of the Media Lab, he was also directly involved in the planning and execution of major events, such as the filming of National Day videos and many events which included the school's Chinese New Year celebrations and orientation programmes which involved live-streaming.

Hong Yi was also one the club's videographers who supported the school's publicity efforts. He was passionate about videography and shared his love of videography with his juniors by imparting his knowledge to them. He was always ready to mentor his juniors, allowing them to develop into proficient photographers and videographers. For his dedication and contribution during his years in the school, he received the Swiss Certificate of Distinction for CCA in 2021.

Hong Yi's compassion for the community was reflected in his active involvement in various Values in Action (VIA) projects. In 2019, Hong Yi took part in an Overseas VIA programme, where he flew to Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam to help in the restoration works of a humanitarian centre, while also organising activities that introduced residents to Singapore's local culture. He also participated in “Care-in-a-pack”, a CCA VIA initiative to collect donated items for the needy during Covid-19 pandemic in 2019 and 2020. Additionally, he contributed greatly to school through media coverage of school events through performing video duties throughout the from 2019-2021.

Hong Yi's zest for learning is apparent and he is always yearning to acquire new knowledge. His independence, self-directedness and ability to adapt quickly will undoubtedly place him in good stead when he faces future challenges.
